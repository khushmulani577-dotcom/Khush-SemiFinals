Step by Step

Cooking Instructions

Follow these easy steps to create your perfect Grilled Paneer Salad

1

Prepare the Paneer Marinade

In a large mixing bowl, combine 1/3 cup curd, ¬Ω TBSP ginger garlic paste, 1 TSP garam masala, ¬º TSP kasuri methi, 1 TSP coriander powder, 2 TSP red chilli powder, ¬Ω TSP black salt, regular salt to taste, ¬Ω TSP lemon juice, and 1 TBSP olive oil. Mix everything thoroughly until you get a smooth, thick marinade.

Prep time: 5 minutes
2

Marinate the Paneer

Cut 150-200 gm paneer into cubes or rectangular pieces. Add the paneer to the marinade and gently coat each piece evenly. Let it marinate for at least 15-20 minutes to absorb all the flavors. For best results, you can refrigerate it for up to 1 hour.

Marinate time: 15-20 minutes
3

Grill the Paneer

Heat a grill pan or regular pan over medium-high heat. Add 1 TBSP olive oil. Once hot, place the marinated paneer pieces on the pan. Grill for 3-4 minutes on each side until you see beautiful golden-brown char marks. The paneer should be slightly crispy on the outside and soft inside. Remove and set aside.

Cook time: 8-10 minutes
4

Prepare Fresh Vegetables

While the paneer cools slightly, prepare all your vegetables. Wash and tear lettuce leaves, cube the bell peppers (use all three colors for a vibrant look), dice the onion and cucumber, grate or julienne the carrots, shred purple cabbage, and halve the cherry tomatoes. Boil the corn if using fresh. Cut pineapple into small cubes and deseed the pomegranate.

Prep time: 10 minutes
5

Make the Dressing

In a small bowl, whisk together ¬º cup curd, 1 TBSP olive oil, 1 TSP lemon juice, 1 TSP balsamic vinegar, ¬Ω TBSP honey, ¬º TSP chat masala, ¬º TSP black salt, regular salt to taste, and ¬º TSP sugar. Whisk vigorously until the dressing becomes smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

Prep time: 3 minutes
6

Assemble the Salad

In a large serving bowl or plate, start with a bed of lettuce leaves. Layer all the prepared vegetables - bell peppers, onions, cucumber, carrots, cabbage, corn, broccoli, and cherry tomatoes. Add the pineapple and pomegranate for a sweet-tart burst. Top with the grilled paneer pieces arranged beautifully. Drizzle the creamy dressing over everything and toss gently just before serving.

Assembly time: 5 minutes

Chef's Secret Tips

Perfect Grilling

Don't overcrowd the pan. Give each paneer piece space to develop those beautiful char marks. Medium-high heat is key!

ü•ó

Keep it Crisp

Add the dressing just before serving to keep vegetables crunchy. You can also serve dressing on the side for individual preferences.

Chill the Bowl

Refrigerate your serving bowl for 15 minutes before assembling. Cold vegetables stay crisp longer and taste more refreshing!

Adjust Spice Level

Control the heat by adjusting red chilli powder. Start with less if you prefer mild flavors - you can always add more!

Use fresh, firm paneer for best results. If paneer is too soft, press it between paper towels to remove excess moisture before marinating.
